# theNORMALschool: Socratic AI Tutor Functional Guidelines

## Overview
At **theNORMALschool**, the Socratic AI Tutor is not an "Answer Machine." [cite_start]It is a sophisticated cognitive scaffold designed to facilitate **Knowledge Building** [cite: 2136] [cite_start]and ensure every student operates within their **Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD)**[cite: 1863, 1936]. 

---

## 1. What the AI Tutor DOES (Student Perspective)

### A. The "Hook" Phase: Launching Inquiry
* [cite_start]**When:** At the start of a new project or "Big Idea"[cite: 2304].
* [cite_start]**Why:** To trigger a "Need-to-Know" situation[cite: 2497].
* [cite_start]**The AI's Action:** It acts as a provocateur, presenting anomalies or data that contradict initial theories to spark curiosity[cite: 2804, 3051]. [cite_start]It asks **facilitative questions** (e.g., "What makes you say that?") to help students externalize their thoughts[cite: 2915, 3021].

### B. The "Mastery" Phase: Scaffolding & Feedback
* [cite_start]**When:** During the practice of specific skills or conceptual building[cite: 2219, 3132].
* [cite_start]**Why:** To provide immediate, non-punitive feedback that allows for **"Productive Failure"**[cite: 3113, 4087, 4102].
* [cite_start]**The AI's Action:** * **Model Tracing:** Follows the student's unique logic path and identifies specific "buggy" productions (misconceptions)[cite: 1989, 2030].
    * [cite_start]**Knowledge Tracing:** Uses Bayesian methods to estimate mastery and adjust the difficulty of tasks automatically[cite: 1990, 2041].
    * [cite_start]**Cognitive Load Management:** Automates rote, repetitive tasks (like basic arithmetic) so the student can focus on higher-order **conceptual understanding**[cite: 2064, 2067, 3112].

### C. The "Guardrail" Phase: Preventing Wheel-Spinning
* [cite_start]**When:** When a student lacks the foundational "chunks" of knowledge required to proceed[cite: 2058, 3128].
* [cite_start]**Why:** To prevent frustration and disengagement that occurs when a task is too far outside the ZPD[cite: 3128, 4299].
* [cite_start]**The AI's Action:** It detects **"Wheel-Spinning"** (effort without progress)[cite: 3128]. [cite_start]It then pivots from Socratic questioning to providing a **"Just-in-Time" module** or worked example[cite: 3113].

### D. The "Synthesis" Phase: Making Thinking Visible
* [cite_start]**When:** When creating final artifacts or reflections[cite: 1586, 2311].
* [cite_start]**Why:** To consolidate learning through **articulation**[cite: 1865, 1944].
* [cite_start]**The AI's Action:** It provides a digital workspace where students are prompted to label and categorize their ideas (e.g., "My Theory," "Evidence For")[cite: 1953, 3249].

## 2. What the AI Tutor DOES NOT (The Human Boundary)

[cite_start]The AI is a technician; the human Guide is the **architect of the Element**[cite: 4124, 4133].

| Feature | Socratic AI Tutor | Human Guide (The Facilitator) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Inspiration** | [cite_start]Cannot "seek to inspire" or ignite passion[cite: 493]. | [cite_start]**Ignites curiosity** based on deep knowledge of the student[cite: 4166]. |
| **MIAP Engine** | [cite_start]No internal **Motivation, Identity, Agency, or Purpose**[cite: 924, 1018]. | [cite_start]Cultivates the student's **"Element"** and life purpose[cite: 3779, 4147]. |
| **Wisdom** | [cite_start]Lacks **Judgment** and the "Golden Mean"[cite: 168, 477]. | [cite_start]Provides **Contextual Wisdom** based on ethics and culture[cite: 347, 4435]. |
| **Empathy** | [cite_start]Simulates empathy via programmed responses[cite: 537, 692]. | [cite_start]Offers **Genuine Empathy** and emotional anchoring[cite: 162, 595]. |
| **Modeling** | [cite_start]Can only model algorithmic processes[cite: 4040]. | [cite_start]Models **Vulnerability** and the human act of failing[cite: 579, 580]. |
