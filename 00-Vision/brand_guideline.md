## I. Brand Identity

### Brand Promise
Help your child discover who they are and what they're capable of—so they can design a life they actually love.

### Core Values (Parent Perspective)
- **Real Preparation**: Your child will be college-ready *and* know who they are
- **Your Child's Agency**: We're not forcing them into a mold; we're helping them find their own shape
- **Honesty Over Hype**: No buzzwords, no shortcuts. Just what works.
- **Community That Matters**: Your child belongs—because we're building a place that sees them

---

## II. Verbal Identity

### Brand Voice
**Audience**: Parents (primary). Your child's future matters to them.
**Tone**: Empathetic, direct, honest. No pedantry. No edu-jargon.
**Personality**: 
- Understands parent anxiety (college, belonging, purpose)
- Speaks to real concerns without defensiveness
- Offers help, not lectures
- Confident in what works, not preachy about methods
- Slightly irreverent (intentional irony in "NORMAL")

### Core Message
Parents want to know: **Will my child be okay? Will they find their place? Will they be ready?**
We say: **Yes—because we're helping them discover who they actually are.**

### Key Messages
1. **"Who they are, not what they know"**: Flips the discovery-vs-compliance paradigm
2. **"Design a life they love"**: Agency and self-knowledge as foundation
3. **"Three promises: love learning, learn by living, live your best life"**: Clear, outcome-focused
4. **Practical reassurance**: UC/A-G aligned, WASC accredited, Mastery Transcript recognized—your child is college-ready

### Language Guidelines
- Use **"your child"** always (never "student," "learner," "individual")
- Lead with the outcome for parents, then explain the method
- Avoid edu-jargon entirely ("leverage," "21st-century skills," "holistic," "synergy")
- Address parent fears directly: college, belonging, purpose, readiness
- Be honest about tradeoffs (if any) rather than overselling
- Embrace the irony in "NORMAL"—don't apologize for it

## VI. Do's & Don'ts

### Language & Messaging
**Do's**
- ✅ Use "your child" (not "student," "learner," "individual")
- ✅ Address parent fears directly (college, belonging, purpose)
- ✅ Lead with outcomes, then explain why
- ✅ Sound empathetic, not preachy
- ✅ Be honest about what we do (and don't do)
- ✅ Include → arrow on all primary CTAs
- ✅ Speak to what parents care about, not what educators think is important

**Don'ts**
- ❌ Use edu-jargon ("leverage," "21st-century skills," "holistic," "synergy," "personalized learning")
- ❌ Lecture parents about pedagogy
- ❌ Apologize for the "NORMAL" name—own the irony
- ❌ Say "student" when you mean "your child"
- ❌ Sound defensive about being different
- ❌ Oversell or use hyperbole ("transform your child's life")
- ❌ Ignore parent objections (college, readiness, belonging)

### Design & Visual
**Do's**
- ✅ Use orange (rgb(255,117,31)) as the primary action/breakthrough color
- ✅ Leverage the "NORMAL" irony directly—"What if..."  messaging
- ✅ Use tight letter-spacing (-0.03em to -0.04em) on headlines for emphasis
- ✅ Include → arrow on all primary CTAs
- ✅ Apply 20px border-radius on cards for soft, approachable feeling
- ✅ Use 88px vertical padding between sections (breathing room)
- ✅ Show real student work and diverse students
- ✅ Keep navigation clean with 8 main sections max

**Don'ts**
- ❌ Use cyan as a primary CTA color (reserved for future interactivity)
- ❌ Use navy instead of orange for heroic/breakthrough moments
- ❌ Over-manipulate images or use generic stock photos
- ❌ Exceed 960px max-width for body content
- ❌ Use button padding less than 6px 11px
- ❌ Mix typography (Inter is the only font)
- ❌ Use weight below 400 or inconsistent weights
- ❌ Create section backgrounds other than white, cream, or orange
- ❌ Avoid whitespace—generous padding is part of the brand

## VII. Responsive Design & Breakpoints

### Breakpoints
- **640px**: Cards and week components stack; images go full-width
- **700px**: Project grids, outcome grids collapse to 1 column
- **760px**: Team section becomes vertical; logos stack
- **800px**: Pricing grid becomes single column; profile cards stack
- **900px**: Desktop nav hidden; hamburger menu appears; 3-column grids become 1 column; experience grids adjust
- **Desktop (900px+)**: Full multi-column layouts; sticky header blur effect active

### Mobile-First Principles
- Stack vertically by default
- Maintain 16–20px horizontal padding on mobile
- Full-width images on cards (no left/right layout)
- Collapse all 3-column grids to single column below 900px
- Week card photos: 180px height on mobile, full photo width
- Hamburger menu appears at 900px breakpoint
- Touch targets: minimum 44–48px for buttons

### Responsive Typography
- **Headlines**: Use `clamp()` for fluid scaling (e.g., clamp(32px, 5.5vw, 72px))
- **Body**: Stay 16px on mobile, don't increase further (readability)
- **Nav**: 13px across all breakpoints
