# Session Log — Element Intake & MIAP Integration

**Date:** June 10, 2026
**Focus:** Defining intake data for the Element hypothesis (ages 10–12) and resolving MIAP's role.

---

## What we discussed

1. What information must be collected about a child (10–12) to draft an Element hypothesis, grounded in Robinson's *The Element* (2009) and *Finding Your Element* (2013).
2. Whether and how Fadel's MIAP (Motivation, Identity, Agency, Purpose) supports finding the child's Element.
3. Whether running a "MIAP assessment" is meaningful at this age.

---

## What we decided

1. **Element intake has four tiers, ordered by necessity.**
   - Tier 1 (irreplaceable): Aptitude signals, Passion signals, Signal mechanics (flow & ease).
   - Tier 2 (development conditions): Attitude, Opportunity/access — pulled up to close the known Robinson gap.
   - Tier 3 (sharpening): Domain breadth, Tribe/social pull, Suppression signals.
   - Tier 4 (context/misread guards): Adult projection risk, Energy patterns.

2. **Aptitude and Passion stay separate signals.** No hypothesis if Tier 1 is incomplete.

3. **MIAP is a validation/development layer, not intake data.** Element identifies *where* potential sits; MIAP observes *whether it takes root and lasts*. Apply MIAP only after a domain signal appears.

4. **No MIAP scoring at ages 10–12.** Drivers are fluid, plural, and pre-conscious (Fadel, 2023). Use presence/absence **observation**, not ratings. A numeric MIAP profile at this age is rejected as false precision.

5. **M → I → A → P is a Guide attention order**, diagnostic priority only — not a developmental claim. M and I are MIAP's real added signal (durability and identity integration); A unblocks misread aptitude; P held loosely.

6. **Authenticity boundary confirmed:** MIAP validates and develops an Element; it must never manufacture one. Engineering Agency or Purpose around an unproven aptitude produces an artificial Element.

---

## What we achieved

- **Created:** `Element-Intake-10-12.md` — four-tier intake list with purpose per item, Guide constraints, and an appended MIAP Observation layer (validation rules + domain-specific observational questions table).
- **Resolved:** the Attitude + Opportunity underweighting in prior intake work (promoted to Tier 2).
- **Resolved:** the "MIAP assessment" question — reframed from scoring to observation.
