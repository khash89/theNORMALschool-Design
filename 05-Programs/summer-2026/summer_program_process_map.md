# Summer Program — Process and Data Map
*What information the program collects, how it is used, and what it produces*

[INTERNAL ONLY]

## Program Purpose
*What the summer program is designed to do*

The Summer Program gives students aged 10–12 six days to build something real. It has two parallel purposes that run simultaneously.

**Visible to the student:** Choose a project, build it with a team, present it to a real audience on Saturday.

**Visible to the Guide only:** Use the week to collect behavioral signals across all four development domains and update each student's Element Hypothesis based on what they reveal through the work.

Every activity in the program serves both purposes at the same time.

## Data Authentication
*How each data type maps to a specific program decision — and where the mapping is reliable*

This section exists for one reason. Before Monday, all data is reported — by parents or students. None of it is observed. The MIAP Design Conditions document is explicit: behavioral diagnosis must come from observation, not self-report. This creates a structural constraint every Guide must hold.

**Rule:** Treat all pre-program data as provisional hypotheses. Monday is the first real data point. Every pre-program assumption is tested on Monday and revised if the live behavior contradicts it.

### Factual Profile Data

| Data Item | Maps To | Reliability | Risk If Wrong |
|---|---|---|---|
| Name and age | Team composition, program administration | High | Low |
| Interests | Project type selection (PBL Skill input) | Medium — parent-reported, subject to aspiration bias | Student selects a project that does not connect to their actual engagement |
| Strengths | Role assignment, Element Hypothesis Natural Pull signal | Medium — "what others notice" is projection-vulnerable | Role misfit discovered on Tuesday when it is too late to reassign easily |
| School background | Agency pre-diagnosis — years of traditional schooling predict learned helplessness | Medium — factual but interpretation varies | Guide underestimates how much Agency-building the student needs |
| Wellbeing notes (current) | Whole Child awareness | Low — "any relevant notes" is not specific enough to act on | Guide is surprised by a student behavior mid-week with no prior context |

### Behavioral Signals

| Data Item | Maps To | Reliability | Risk If Wrong |
|---|---|---|---|
| Type of problems they gravitate toward | Element Hypothesis Natural Pull, project type selection | Medium — self-reported, but one of the more reliable signals | Project misfit |
| Response to challenge | Agency MIAP diagnosis | Low — parent-reported behavior in non-school contexts may not generalize | Guide designs wrong Agency conditions or skips them entirely |
| Team vs. solo preference | Format selection | High — students and parents usually know this accurately | Solo student placed in team, or team student working alone — both reduce engagement |
| Energy: engaged vs. compliant | Motivation MIAP diagnosis | Low — parents often cannot distinguish genuine engagement from performance of engagement | Guide misjudges Motivation driver strength, embeds wrong conditions |

### Missing Data — Added in This Version

| Missing Data | Why It Was Absent | What It Enables |
|---|---|---|
| Prior making history | Not collected in original screening | ZPD calibration — Guide sets sprint tasks at the right difficulty level |
| Specific wellbeing baseline | Vague "any relevant notes" placeholder | Guide knows in advance how to respond to this specific student in difficulty |
| Social dynamics | Not collected at all | Team composition that avoids known conflict and supports social wellbeing |
| Parent expectations | Collected in parent screening but not used in process map | Guide frames Saturday outcomes to match what this parent is watching for |
| Learning working preferences | Not collected at all | Guide calibrates between structured prompts and open-ended space per student |

## Phase 1 — Pre-Program
*What is collected before Monday, and how it is used*

### 1.1 Student Screening
*Building the initial picture of each student*

Each family completes a student screening before enrollment. The screening collects five categories of information.

**Category A — Factual profile:**
- Student name and age
- Interests: what they do voluntarily, talk about, or lose time doing (minimum 2 specific examples)
- Strengths: observable behaviors and skills — not generic descriptors like "smart" or "creative"

**Category B — Prior making history:**
- What is the most complex thing this student has ever built, made, written, or produced?
- Have they worked in a team before? What happened?
- What have they tried and abandoned? Why?
- This data tells the Guide what the student's baseline capability actually looks like — not what parents hope it is.
- Used for: ZPD calibration (setting sprint tasks at the edge of capability, not above or below it)

**Category C — Wellbeing baseline:**
- How does this student handle frustration specifically — not challenge in general?
- Are there any diagnosed conditions, sensory sensitivities, or accommodations the Guide should know about?
- Any significant recent life events (loss, family change, school transition)?
- What does anxiety look like for this student, if applicable?
- Used for: Guide awareness plan — the Guide knows before Monday what to watch for and how to respond

**Category D — Social dynamics:**
- Does this student know anyone else enrolled in the same session?
- How do they handle disagreement with peers?
- Are they more comfortable leading, contributing, or following in group situations?
- Any known peer conflicts to avoid?
- Used for: Team composition — prevents conflict risk, supports social wellbeing from Day 1

**Category E — Parent expectations and working preferences:**
- What does the parent hope this student gets from the week?
- What have well-meaning adults gotten wrong about this child?
- Does the student prefer to plan before starting, or prototype and figure it out as they go?
- Do they work better with detailed structure or with open space?
- Used for: Saturday communication strategy (what the Guide tells this parent), Guide response protocol calibration, projection risk identification

The screening uses two documents depending on age and situation:
- **Student Hypothesis Screening Questions** — for students ages 11–12 completing it themselves
- **Parent Screening Questions (Ages 10–12)** — for parents completing it on behalf of younger or less self-aware students

### 1.2 Initial Element Hypothesis
*A provisional picture of who the student is — tested and revised on Monday*

A Guide drafts the Initial Element Hypothesis after reviewing the screening data. It is not a diagnosis. It is a starting hypothesis built from reported data that Monday observations will either confirm or revise.

The hypothesis contains:
- Natural Pull: what type of work or problem the student gravitates toward
- Motivation signal: what generates genuine energy versus compliance
- Identity signal: how the student describes themselves, what role feels natural
- Agency signal: how the student handles decisions and obstacles
- Purpose signal: whether the student connects their work to others

The hypothesis is not shared with the student. It is a Guide tool.

**Thin but usable:** A hypothesis with limited signals — "might be drawn to building things" — is enough to proceed. Generate projects across all three types and let Monday resolve the direction.

**Not usable:** Screening data contradicts itself, or wellbeing data is unresolved. Guide contacts the family before the week starts.

### 1.3 Project Matching
*Personalizing the six pre-designed projects for this student — then letting them choose*

The Guide uses the **PBL Project Designer** skill with the student's full profile and Initial Element Hypothesis as inputs. The skill does not generate custom projects. It scores all six library projects against the student's data, selects the 3–5 most strongly matched, and personalizes each one using the library's personalization layer.

**What "strongly matched" means:**
A project scores strongly matched when the student's Natural Pull and Identity signal align directly with at least one role in that project, their stated interests connect to the project's domain, and the project's structure can address at least one of the student's weak MIAP drivers.

**What the skill outputs per selected project:**
- A personalized driving question (selected from the library's question variants based on the student's hypothesis signal type)
- The recommended role for this student (with the signal it matches, named)
- The other team roles (briefly described)
- A one-to-two sentence explanation of why this project fits this student specifically
- Saturday showcase moment
- Guide-facing configuration: element exposure moments, MIAP conditions, Four Domains mapping

**What the student sees:**
The student-facing section only. No framework names. No mention of element hypothesis, MIAP, or Four Domains. The student sees projects that feel tailored to them — not a clinical assessment.

**Who chooses:**
The student chooses from the curated set. The Guide does not express a preference. The choice itself is the first Agency signal of the program.

The project selection captures:
- Which project (from the curated set)
- Format confirmation (Team — primary / Solo — available)
- Role preference (optional — Guide confirms roles on Monday after live observation)

### 1.4 MIAP Pre-Diagnosis
*Identifying which conditions to embed — subject to Monday revision*

Before Monday, the Guide reviews each student's Initial Element Hypothesis for MIAP driver strength signals. Every diagnosis at this stage is provisional.

| Driver | What the Guide checks from screening | Provisional diagnosis limit |
|---|---|---|
| Motivation | Does the hypothesis show voluntary engagement, or only compliance signals? | Based on parent report — confirmed by Tuesday behavior |
| Identity | Can the student describe themselves as a learner? Or only by negation? | Based on self-report — confirmed by Monday role behavior |
| Agency | Does the student initiate, or wait for permission? | Based on reported behavior — confirmed by Tuesday deep work |
| Purpose | Does the student connect their work to others, or only to personal interest? | Based on report — confirmed by Wednesday feedback response |

For any driver that appears absent or weak, the Guide plans which design condition to embed into the week. These conditions are locked in after Monday observation — not before.

### 1.5 Pre-Monday Team Composition
*Building teams with intention*

For team projects, the Guide assembles teams before Monday using four inputs:

1. Project selections (who chose the same project)
2. Element Hypothesis signals (which role fits each student's Natural Pull)
3. Social dynamics data (who knows each other, who should not be on the same team)
4. Working preferences (mix of planning-first and prototype-first students within one team creates friction — balance it)

Roles are tentative before Monday. The Guide confirms them during Monday orientation after observing the cohort together. A role assignment that looks right from the screening may look wrong within the first two hours of Monday.

### 1.6 Guide Readiness Check
*What the Guide reviews before the week begins*

The Guide completes this check before Monday. It is not optional.

| Check | Pass condition | Action if not passed |
|---|---|---|
| Initial Element Hypothesis formed for every student | All students have at least a directional signal | Contact family. Do not start the week without a hypothesis. |
| MIAP pre-diagnosis complete for every student | At least one driver identified as weak or absent per student | Revisit screening data. If still unclear, Monday observation resolves it. |
| Team compositions finalized with intention | Every team has role diversity, no known conflict pairings | Revise composition before Monday. |
| Wellbeing baseline reviewed for every student | No unresolved flags | Escalate any unresolved wellbeing concern before Monday. Do not enter the week with an unaddressed flag. |
| Parent expectations noted for every student | Guide knows what each parent is watching for | Use for Saturday communication framing. |
| ZPD baseline estimated for every student | Guide has a rough sense of each student's capability level based on prior making history | Use for sprint task calibration on Monday. |

## Phase 2 — Program Week
*How data is collected and used across each day*

### Monday — Orientation, Team Forming, and Hypothesis Testing

Monday has two visible purposes (orientation and team forming) and one invisible one: testing every pre-program hypothesis against live behavior.

**Morning inputs used:**
- Student profiles, Initial Element Hypotheses, pre-assigned teams and tentative roles

**Morning process:**
- Workshop: collaboration — how to divide work so the whole is stronger than the parts
- Cohort gets to know each other
- Teams form, roles confirmed with Guide observation

**Afternoon process:**
- Team aligns on problem angle and Sprint 1 target
- Each role defines specific responsibilities
- Team commits to a shared weekly plan

**Guide — Monday live observation (hypothesis testing):**

| What to observe | What it tests | Revision trigger |
|---|---|---|
| How the student enters the room and connects with peers | Social dynamics data accuracy | If screening said "shy" but student is immediately confident, revise |
| Which role the student moves toward naturally vs. waits to be assigned | Identity and Agency pre-diagnosis | If student self-directs role choice, Agency pre-diagnosis may be too low |
| Energy level during free cohort time vs. during structured sprint planning | Motivation pre-diagnosis | Different energy levels = Motivation signal confirmed. Same flat energy = deeper issue |
| How the student responds when the team disagrees on the problem angle | Agency and Purpose signals | Student who defers immediately = Agency condition needed. Student who advocates = Agency stronger than reported |

**Monday output:**
- Team formed · Roles confirmed · Sprint 1 target committed
- Guide observation: Day 1 baseline across four domains
- **Revised MIAP pre-diagnosis** — Guide updates condition plan based on live Monday signals before Tuesday begins

---

### Tuesday — Sprint 1, Day 1

**Morning inputs used:**
- Sprint 1 plan · Daily results commitment · Revised MIAP conditions from Monday

**Morning process:**
- Workshop: planning under constraints — how to set a target you can actually hit today
- Team sprint planning · Each role defines Day 1 output

**Afternoon process:**
- Each role builds their component
- Team integrates components
- First version of project complete

**Data collected by Guide:**
- Observation across four domains (notes, not ratings yet)
- MIAP signals per domain: which drivers are active, which are absent
- ZPD check: is the sprint task too easy (no agency required) or too hard (frustration shuts down effort)?
- Agency signal: does the student initiate, or wait for permission before every step?
- Motivation signal: does energy change when the task type changes?

**Tuesday output:**
- First integrated version of the project
- Guide observation: Day 2 behavioral signals per domain

---

### Wednesday — Sprint 1, Day 2 (Feedback Day)

**Morning inputs used:**
- First version of the project · Daily results commitment

**Morning process:**
- Workshop: testing with real people — how to learn from a user without leading them
- Team tests first version with 3–5 real people
- Each role documents feedback on their component

**Feedback session data collected:**
- What worked (user confirmed)
- What did not work (user identified)
- What surprised the team
- Each role's component feedback documented separately

**Afternoon process:**
- Team synthesizes feedback together
- Identifies top 3 improvements for Sprint 2
- Commits to Sprint 2 plan

**Guide check-in (Wednesday midpoint):**
- Named behavioral observation per student — not evaluation
- Example: "I notice that when you hit a structural problem, you go quiet in a way that looks like concentration, not avoidance."
- This is a designed Identity condition. See MIAP Design Conditions document.
- Purpose signal check: does the student connect improvement decisions to the end user, or only to personal preference?

**Wednesday output:**
- Feedback synthesized · Sprint 2 plan committed
- Guide observation: Day 3 MIAP signals updated per domain
- Named behavioral observation delivered to each student

---

### Thursday — Sprint 2, Day 1

**Morning inputs used:**
- Sprint 2 plan · Synthesized feedback from Wednesday · Daily results commitment

**Morning process:**
- Workshop: taking feedback without losing your vision — how to decide what to act on and what to ignore
- Team sprint planning for Sprint 2 · Each role redesigns their component

**Afternoon process:**
- Each role rebuilds their component
- Team integrates improved version
- Improved version tested · final feedback collected

**Data collected by Guide:**
- Agency signal: does the student attempt the rebuild before asking for help?
- Purpose signal: does the student connect what they are improving to the person it helps?
- Wellbeing check: any student showing signs of fatigue, frustration, or social friction that needs attention before Friday?

**Thursday output:**
- Improved version tested · final feedback collected
- Guide observation: Day 4 signals per domain

---

### Friday — Sprint 2, Day 2 (Finalization)

**Morning inputs used:**
- Improved version · All Guide observations Mon–Thu · Daily results commitment

**Morning process:**
- Workshop: presenting to a cold audience — how to make someone care before you explain what it is
- Each role finalizes their component · Team integrates final version

**Afternoon process:**
- Team prepares showcase materials
- Full team rehearsal
- Guide review: formal assessment across all four domains

**Guide review — Four Domains assessment:**

| Domain | What the Guide assesses | Evidence source |
|---|---|---|
| The Element | Natural Pull signals · MIAP driver strength per domain — compared against initial pre-diagnosis | Mon–Fri observations + student behavior during work |
| Knowledge | What subject knowledge the student pulled and applied — compared against baseline from prior making history | Project output + conversations during deep work |
| Timeless Competencies | Which competencies the student practiced — with specific behavioral evidence, not impression | Sprint behavior, feedback response, rehearsal behavior |
| Whole Child Development | Wellbeing signals: social, emotional, mental, physical — compared against wellbeing baseline from screening | Daily observations, peer interactions, energy patterns across the week |

**Friday output:**
- Final version complete · Presentation ready
- Guide assessment: draft Four Domains review per student
- Guide note: how to frame Saturday outcomes for each parent based on parent expectations data

## Phase 3 — Saturday
*Showcase and closing data collection*

### Morning
- Final setup · Full team rehearsal
- Guide check: any last wellbeing or dynamics observations before the public event

### Showcase
- Team presents to real audience (parents, guests, community)
- Audience gives structured feedback
- Every student presents — no exceptions

### Element Hypothesis Reflection
*The student's contribution to their own updated hypothesis*

After presenting, each student submits a written or spoken reflection. Three questions only:

1. What did you discover about the kind of work that gives you energy this week?
2. What did you do that surprised you — something you did not expect to be good at or care about?
3. Who is better off because of what you built?

This reflection is the student's input to the updated Element Hypothesis. The Guide does not show the student the original hypothesis. The reflection stands on its own.

### Guide Finalizes Assessment
- Incorporates showcase behavior and reflection into the Four Domains assessment
- Updates MIAP profile per domain based on the full week of observed signals
- Drafts Updated Element Hypothesis
- Notes any parent communication framing needed based on parent expectations data

## Data Relationships
*How inputs become outputs*

```
SCREENING DATA (reported — provisional)
├── Factual profile + prior making history
│     ↓
│   Project Matching (5 options) → Student selects 1
│
├── Behavioral signals
│     ↓
│   Initial Element Hypothesis (provisional)
│     ↓
│   MIAP Pre-Diagnosis (provisional)
│
├── Wellbeing baseline
│     ↓
│   Guide Awareness Plan (what to watch for per student)
│
├── Social dynamics
│     ↓
│   Team Composition (conflict-aware, role-intentional)
│
└── Parent expectations
      ↓
    Saturday Communication Strategy (per parent)

GUIDE READINESS CHECK
    ↓ (all provisional data confirmed or flagged before Monday)

MONDAY: Live observation → Hypothesis Testing
    ↓
Revised Element Hypothesis + Revised MIAP Conditions
    ↓
TUESDAY: Sprint 1 built → ZPD check → MIAP signals observed
    ↓
WEDNESDAY: Real feedback → Synthesized → Sprint 2 plan
            Named behavioral observation (Identity condition)
    ↓
THURSDAY: Rebuild → Agency + Purpose signals observed
    ↓
FRIDAY: Final version → Four Domains assessment drafted
    ↓
SATURDAY: Public presentation → Student reflection submitted
    ↓
OUTPUTS (all based on observed behavior, not report):
    → Completed project artifact
    → Updated Element Hypothesis (observed, not reported)
    → Four Domains assessment (evidence-cited)
    → Updated MIAP profile (domain-specific)
    → Parent communication notes
```
