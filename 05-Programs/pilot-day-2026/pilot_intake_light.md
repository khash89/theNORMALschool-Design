# Pilot Day — Light Intake
*Short pre-day questionnaire for pilot families — ages 10–12*

[INTERNAL ONLY — form content below is parent/student-facing]

---

## Purpose

A compressed version of the full Element intake ([Element-Intake-10-12.md](../summer-2026/Element-Intake-10-12.md)), covering **Tier 1 signals only** — Aptitude, Passion, and flow — plus project preference. It exists to:

1. Guide project and role assignment before the pilot day
2. Give the Guide a per-student baseline for the observation cards
3. Test whether a light intake predicts in-day signals well enough — or whether the full intake is genuinely necessary

**Rules carried over from the full intake:**
- Aptitude and Passion are recorded as **separate signals** — never merged
- Ask for specific, behavioral answers — *"draws machines and how they connect,"* not *"good at art"*
- Identification stays invisible to the child: the student questions are framed as preferences, not assessment

**Send:** at least 1 week before the pilot day. **Time to complete:** ~10 minutes.

---

## Part 1 — Parent Questions

**1. Aptitude — what comes unusually easily?**
Describe 1–2 specific things your child picks up faster or does with more ease than you'd expect for their age. Be concrete — what exactly do they do, and how do you know it comes easily?

**2. Passion — what do they return to without being asked?**
What does your child do voluntarily, repeatedly, with no reward and no prompting? When did you last see them do it?

**3. Flow — when do they lose track of time?**
Is there an activity where your child resists stopping, forgets meals, or has to be called multiple times? What is it?

**4. The free-choice check.**
On a free afternoon with no screens-by-default and nothing scheduled, what does your child actually end up doing?

**5. Anything we shouldn't misread?**
Anything about your child's day, energy, or social style that would help an adult read them accurately in a 4-hour session? (e.g., slow to warm up, energized by groups, frustrated when interrupted mid-task)

---

## Part 2 — Student Questions
*Framed as preferences. No right answers. Parent may scribe, but answers must be the child's own words.*

**6. If you could spend tomorrow making one thing, what would it be?**

**7. Pick the one that sounds most fun:**
- a) Being on camera and telling a story people actually watch
- b) Filming and editing a video until it feels exactly right
- c) Making the rules of a game and deciding how it works
- d) Building the world, characters, and look of a game

**8. What's something you made (anything — a video, a Minecraft build, a comic, a recipe, a fort) that you were proud of? What was the best part of making it?**

**9. When you work on something with other people, what part do you usually grab first?**

## Part 3 — Guide Processing
*[INTERNAL ONLY — completed by the Guide from the returned form]*

| Field | Entry |
|---|---|
| **Aptitude signals (specific)** | |
| **Passion signals (specific)** | |
| **Flow evidence** | |
| **Aptitude/Passion alignment** | Same domain · Different domains · Unclear |
| **Project match** | YouTube Studio · Game Builder — with one-line rationale against the match profiles in [summer_project_library.md](../summer-2026/summer_project_library.md) |
| **Role assignment** | Role + the signal that drove it (role-to-signal guidance from the library) |
| **Prediction to test** | One sentence: the signal the Guide expects to see confirmed on pilot day |
| **Watch-for** | Anything from Q5 that shapes how to read this student |

**Processing rules:**
- Q7 maps directly to roles: (a) Host/Presenter · (b) Director/Editor · (c) Game Designer · (d) World/Art Builder. Treat it as the child's self-report, to be weighed against — not above — the parent's behavioral evidence.
- If parent answers are achievement-framed with no free-choice behavior (adult projection risk), flag it and weight Part 2 higher.
- If Tier 1 signals are thin across the board, do not force a confident match — assign by Q7 preference and mark the card *"low-confidence baseline."* That student's pilot data is about what the intake missed.
- The **Prediction to test** field is mandatory. The post-pilot intake validation (see [pilot_day_program.md](pilot_day_program.md), After the Day) depends on it.
