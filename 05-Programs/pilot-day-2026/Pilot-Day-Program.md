# Pilot Day — One-Day PBL Program
*Half-day internal pilot of the summer program model — ages 10–12*

[INTERNAL ONLY]

---

## Program Overview

- **Purpose:** Internal pilot — test the program mechanics, the Guide observation protocol, and one-day-scoped project briefs before the summer launch
- **Duration:** One half-day session (~4 hours)
- **Proposed window:** Before June 29, 2026 (summer program start) — date TBD
- **Hours:** 9:00 am – 1:00 pm
- **Cohort:** 2 teams · 2–3 members each (4–6 students total)
- **Ages:** 10–12
- **Project menu:** 2 one-day-scoped options — The YouTube Studio (Storytelling) · The Game Builder (Web App Building)
- **Project selection:** Before the day, guided by the light intake (see [Pilot-Intake-Light.md](Pilot-Intake-Light.md))
- **Ending:** Internal share-out — teams present to each other and the Guide. No external audience.
- **Pricing:** Free — pilot participants are invited families

---

## What This Pilot Tests
*The pilot is an instrument. These are the questions it must answer.*

| # | Question | Evidence to Collect |
|---|----------|--------------------|
| 1 | Does the daily rhythm hold at half scale? | Actual vs. planned block timings · where energy drops · which transitions leak time |
| 2 | Does morning results-setting + end-of-day accountability work with this age? | Were commitments concrete? Did the close honestly compare output to commitment? |
| 3 | Does a universal workshop topic serve both project types simultaneously? | Did both teams apply the workshop content within the hour that followed? |
| 4 | Can one Guide track 4 domains across 2 teams during sprint blocks? | Observation cards completed per student · gaps in coverage · protocol friction |
| 5 | Do the briefs scope to a single day? | Did teams reach a finished, presentable artifact? Where did scope break? |
| 6 | Do diagnostic moments surface usable Element signals in compressed form? | Signals logged at the two moments vs. light-intake predictions |

## Half-Day Schedule

The day is one full compressed loop of the summer model: commit → learn → build → test → improve → present → reflect.

| Time | Block | What Happens |
|------|-------|--------------|
| **9:00–9:15** | Unorganized fun | Free movement, games, music. No agenda, no adult direction. Body and mood lifted before the day begins. |
| **9:15–9:30** | Results setting | Teams confirm their project and roles (chosen before the day). Each team commits to one concrete deliverable for 12:30, written and posted visibly. |
| **9:30–9:50** | Micro-workshop | *Planning under constraints — how to set a target you can actually hit today.* Universal topic, serves both project types. 20 minutes, then straight into application. |
| **9:50–11:10** | Deep work — Sprint 1 | Each role builds their component. Team integrates. Target: a rough but complete first version by 11:10. Guide circulates with observation cards. |
| **11:10–11:25** | Break + snack | Compressed nourishment block. Students serve and clean up together. |
| **11:25–11:45** | Cross-team testing | Each team plays/watches the other team's work and gives structured feedback: *what worked, what confused you, what's missing.* Replaces the external audience test of the full program. |
| **11:45–12:30** | Deep work — Sprint 2 | Teams apply feedback and finalize. Guide prompts each team to name the top 2 improvements before touching the work. |
| **12:30–12:50** | Internal share-out | Each team presents the finished artifact to the other team and the Guide: what they built, what changed after feedback, what they'd do with five more days. |
| **12:50–13:00** | Reflection + close | Each student compares output against the 9:15 commitment — honestly, no softening. One question each: *"When today did you lose track of time?"* |

## Project Menu — One-Day Cuts
*Two options, one per type. Each team picks one before the day. Both teams may pick the same project.*

Both are compressed cuts of the summer library briefs ([Summer-Project-Library.md](../summer-2026/design/Summer-Project-Library.md)). The fixed core shrinks; the personalization layer and diagnostic logic carry over intact.

### Fixed Core

| | The YouTube Studio — One-Day Cut | The Game Builder — One-Day Cut |
|---|---|---|
| **Type** | Storytelling | Web App Building |
| **Builds** | A finished 60–90 second video with a hook, a story, and a payoff — shot, edited, and watchable by 12:30. | A playable browser game with one original mechanic and one complete level — playable by someone else by 12:30. |
| **Roles (3)** | Host/Presenter · Director/Editor · Thumbnail & Graphics Designer | Game Designer (mechanics + story) · World/Art Builder · Developer |
| **Roles (2-member fallback)** | Host/Presenter · Director/Editor (thumbnail folded into Editor) | Game Designer · Builder (art + development merged) |
| **Tools** | Smartphone camera · CapCut or iMovie · Canva (thumbnail) | Scratch (ages 10–11) · GDevelop (ages 11–12) |
| **Scope guardrails** | One location · max 3 shots per scene · script locked by end of Sprint 1 — no reshoots of working footage in Sprint 2 | One level · one mechanic · no character animation beyond movement — polish only in Sprint 2, no new features |
| **Share-out format** | Screening: the other team watches cold, rates the hook in the first 10 seconds, then the team explains their choices. | Play session: the other team plays without instructions, then the team explains every design decision out loud. |

### Personalization Layer

The match profiles, driving question variants, and role-to-signal guidance from the summer library apply unchanged — use the **YouTube Studio** and **Game Builder** sections of [Summer-Project-Library.md](../summer-2026/design/Summer-Project-Library.md), fed by the light intake instead of the full Element Hypothesis.

### Diagnostic Moments — Compressed Mapping

The two diagnostic moments per project survive compression. They move to fixed blocks:

| Project | Type | Block | What to Watch | Signal It Surfaces |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| YouTube Studio | **Confirmation** | Sprint 1 — hook writing | Does the student write the opening for the audience, or for their own interest? | Audience-first = communicator/performer Identity |
| YouTube Studio | **Discovery** | Cross-team testing | Does the student watch the viewers' faces, or their own work on screen? | Watching the audience = empathy + Purpose emerging |
| Game Builder | **Confirmation** | Sprint 1 — mechanics design | Does the student gravitate to rules (systems) or world and story (creative)? | Splits Natural Pull into a more precise signal |
| Game Builder | **Discovery** | Cross-team testing | Does the student analyze play patterns (systems) or read the player's feelings (empathy)? | Empathy signal = Purpose emerging |

## Guide Protocol
*One Guide, two teams, four domains — at pilot scale this must be provably doable.*

### Observation Cards

One card per student, prepared before the day with the student's light-intake summary at the top. The Guide logs short, timestamped, verbatim-where-possible notes in four rows:

| Domain | What to Log |
|---|---|
| **The Element** | Aptitude and Passion signals separately — flow states, unprompted returns, ease. Note where signals match or contradict the light intake. |
| **Knowledge** | What the student needed to learn to move forward, and whether they pulled it themselves or waited to be fed. |
| **Timeless Competencies** | One observed behavior per logged competency — not a rating, an incident. |
| **Whole Child** | Energy patterns across blocks · social dynamics in the team · response to the feedback round. |

### Circulation Rule

During each sprint block the Guide completes one full pass per team per 30 minutes, anchored on the diagnostic moments. Observe, ask questions, surface what the student cannot see — never direct the work.

### Pilot Operations Log

Separate from student observation — one page logging the *model itself*: actual block timings, transition friction, workshop landing, scope breaks, and anything that would not survive 20 students. This page is the pilot's primary deliverable.

## After the Day
*The pilot is not finished at 1:00 pm.*

1. **Same day — Guide debrief (30 min):** Walk the six pilot questions against the operations log while memory is fresh.
2. **Within 48 hours — written findings:** One page per pilot question: answer, evidence, and the change it forces in the summer design.
3. **Update the summer documents:** Push confirmed changes into [Summer-Program-Project-Arc.md](../summer-2026/design/Summer-Program-Project-Arc.md) and [Summer-Project-Library.md](../summer-2026/design/Summer-Project-Library.md). The pilot has failed if it produces no edits.
4. **Light-intake validation:** Compare each student's in-day signals against their light-intake summary. Where the intake predicted well and where it missed informs the full Element Hypothesis process.

## Notes
*Design decisions made for the pilot*

[INTERNAL ONLY]

**One loop, not a shrunken week.** The half day runs the full summer loop once — commit, learn, build, test, improve, present, reflect — rather than sampling fragments of it. The pilot tests the loop's integrity, not its endurance.

**Cross-team testing replaces the external audience.** With no real users available in four hours, each team is the other team's audience. This is weaker evidence than strangers but preserves the mechanic being tested: building for someone who owes you nothing, then watching them react. The Discovery diagnostic moments attach here.

**Internal share-out keeps the pilot low-stakes by design.** The showcase mechanic at full scale is a separate question; this pilot deliberately does not test it. Do not generalize share-out findings to Saturday showcase behavior.

**Scope guardrails are load-bearing.** A six-day brief compressed to one day fails by scope, not by concept. The guardrails (one level, one mechanic; one location, locked script) are part of the brief, stated to students at results setting, and enforced by the Guide at the Sprint 2 boundary.

**Light intake is itself under test.** The pilot doubles as a dry run of signal-based project matching with a fraction of the full intake burden. Tier 1 signals only (Aptitude, Passion, flow) plus project preference — see [Pilot-Intake-Light.md](Pilot-Intake-Light.md).

**Teams of 2 need merged roles, not dropped ones.** With 2–3 members, the 2-member fallback merges adjacent roles rather than removing a function. Every function in the fixed core must still be owned by someone, or the brief being tested is no longer the summer brief.
