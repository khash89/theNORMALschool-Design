## 1. Program Overview

- **Purpose:** Run one full compressed loop of the summer model — *commit → learn → build → test → improve → present → reflect* — in a single afternoon at home, and surface Yesui's Element signals through the work.
- **Duration:** One afternoon session — **12:00 pm – 6:00 pm (6 hours)**
- **Setting:** Home (kitchen, a play space, and a backyard/living-room sports area)
- **Format:** Solo project with a **family crew**. Yesui is the Director. Her brothers hold defined supporting roles. Her parents are the **fresh showcase audience** — they do not direct the day.
- **Project:** *The Sparkle Squad Celebration* — a custom age-8 brief (see [§4](#4-project-brief)) adapted from **The Food Business** (Entrepreneurship) crossed with a **Storytelling/Showcase** ending.
- **Ending:** A live **showcase to her parents** at 5:30 — present what she made, give handmade gifts, demo a move, then reflect.
- **Model basis:** [`pilot_day_program.md`](../05-Programs/pilot-day-2026/pilot_day_program.md) (one-day compressed loop) · [`summer_program_project_arc.md`](../05-Programs/summer-2026/summer_program_project_arc.md) (daily rhythm, Guide role, showcase) · [`summer_project_library.md`](../05-Programs/summer-2026/summer_project_library.md) (brief format) · [`09-Appendix-Four-Domains.md`](../01-School-Model/09-Appendix-Four-Domains.md) (what is being developed).

### Age calibration note
The summer and pilot programs target ages 10–12. Yesui is **8**. This plan deliberately scales the model down: shorter blocks, a single concrete artifact per block, a trusted adult holding the Guide role *lightly*, a 3-sentence reflection instead of written analysis, and a family audience instead of strangers. The loop and the Four-Domains intent are preserved; the load is not.

### *The Sparkle Squad Celebration*

### Personalization Layer
*Used to tailor the brief to Yesui specifically.*

**Match Profile**

| Signal Type | Matched Signals |
|---|---|
| Strongest match | Natural Pull: hands-on making (cooking, bracelets) · Purpose: creating something for people she loves · Identity: host/leader who brings people together |
| Secondary match | Physical expression (gymnastics, running, soccer) · Performer energy (glitter, Kalogera sisters) |

**Driving Question** — *Purpose/host-first variant chosen to match her family-centered Purpose signal:*

> ### "How can I bring my family together with the things I love — and show Mom and Dad what I made?"

| Variant | Use when | Question |
|---|---|---|
| Host/Purpose-first *(selected)* | Purpose + family signal strong | "How can I bring my family together with the things I love — and show Mom and Dad what I made?" |
| Maker-first | Craft focus dominates | "What can I make today that my whole family will love?" |
| Performer-first | Host/performer energy dominates | "What show could I put on that my family will never forget?" |

**Role-to-Signal Guidance** *(for the Director seat — the others are fixed by age/family)*

| If Yesui shows… | It signals… |
|---|---|
| Focus on the *making* (perfecting the recipe, the bead pattern) | Maker / artisan identity |
| Focus on *who receives it* (imagining her family's reaction) | Host / purpose-driven identity |
| Focus on *leading the crew* (assigning, directing, coaching) | Leader / organizer identity |
| Focus on *the show* (the bow, the reveal, the performance) | Performer identity |

### Diagnostic Moments
*Two built-in observation points. Invisible to Yesui — she is just doing the project. The Guide is watching.*

| Type | Block | What happens | What to watch | Signal it surfaces |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Confirmation** | Bracelets (1:45) | She designs and threads a bracelet for each family member | Does her focus go to the **craft itself** (the pattern, the glitter) or to **imagining each person wearing it**? | Craft-focused = maker identity confirmed · Person-focused = Purpose/host identity confirmed |
| **Discovery** | Showtime (5:30) | She presents to her parents — a fresh audience | Does she **come alive presenting** (performer + social confidence), or prefer to **let the objects speak** (maker/introvert)? | Performer/social-confidence signal — frequently absent from the initial hypothesis; either result updates it |

## 5. The One-Day Loop & Daily Rhythm
*The full summer loop, run once, scaled to 6 hours. Same rhythm as the model: unorganized fun → results setting → micro-workshop → deep work (with nourishment folded in) → testing → improve → showcase → reflection.*

| Time | Block | Loop phase | What happens |
|---|---|---|---|
| **12:00–12:15** | Unorganized fun | — | Free play/movement with her brothers. No agenda. Body and mood lifted before the day begins. |
| **12:15–12:30** | Results setting | **Commit** | Yesui commits to her 5:30 deliverables (snack, bracelets, course, show), assigns crew roles, draws her plan poster. *First Agency signal.* |
| **12:30–12:45** | Micro-workshop | **Learn** | Universal age-8 skill: *"How to make a plan you can actually finish."* 15 min, then straight into application. |
| **12:45–13:45** | Deep work + Nourishment | **Build** | **Station 2 — Sparkle Chef Kitchen.** Cook the celebration snack together; this doubles as the nourishment hour (they eat together). |
| **13:45–14:45** | Deep work | **Build** | **Station 3 — Glitter Friendship Bracelets.** One per family member. *← Confirmation diagnostic.* |
| **14:45–15:30** | Deep work | **Build** | **Station 4 — Build the Sparkle Sports Course** (gymnastics + soccer + running; toddler zone for Little Bro). |
| **15:30–16:15** | Testing | **Test** | **Station 5 — Family Sports Games.** The crew runs the course; she coaches and times it — testing what she built. |
| **16:15–17:00** | Deep work | **Improve** | **Station 6 — Make the Showcase.** Fix what didn't work, lay out artifacts in order, rehearse the presentation. |
| **17:00–17:30** | Reset + rehearse | **Improve** | Snack/rest, then final rehearsal of her 3 sentences. *Self-Mastery before a public moment.* |
| **17:30–18:00** | Showcase + Reflection | **Present → Reflect** | **Station 7 — Showtime for Mom & Dad.** Present, give gifts, demo a move, bow. Then the 3-question reflection. *← Discovery diagnostic.* |

> **Stations 1 and 6–7** correspond to the kid-facing poster's stations. The model blocks above (unorganized fun, results setting, micro-workshop, reflection) are the **Guide-facing wrapper** around the 7 student-facing stations in [§6](#6-student-facing-schedule).

## 8. Guide Protocol
*One adult, held lightly, family setting. The Guide observes, asks, and surfaces — never directs the work.*

- **Circulation:** Check in once per station. Anchor attention on the two diagnostic moments (bracelets, showtime).
- **Observation card — four rows** (kept privately; one card for the day):

| Domain | What to log |
|---|---|
| The Element | Aptitude & Passion separately — where she enters flow, what she returns to unprompted. Note where signals match or contradict §3. |
| Knowledge | What she needed to figure out to move forward — did she pull it herself or wait to be fed? |
| Timeless Competencies | One observed *incident* per competency — not a rating. |
| Whole Child | Energy across blocks · how she leads Little Bro · how she handles a setback. |

- **The not-direct rule:** When she's stuck, ask before telling — *"What's your plan for that?"* The struggle is where Agency is built.
- **Parents stay fresh:** Mom & Dad are the showcase audience. Keep them out of the work so 5:30 is a real first reveal — this protects the Discovery diagnostic.
- **A named observation (optional, age-appropriate):** one warm, specific noticing during the day — e.g., *"I noticed you kept checking how Little Bro was doing while you worked."* Surfaces self-awareness without evaluating.

## 9. The Showcase & Reflection

**Showcase (17:30–18:00):** Yesui welcomes Mom & Dad, walks them through her three artifacts in order (cook → bracelets → sports), gives each a bracelet gift, demonstrates one sports move, and takes a bow. Every artifact is real. The showcase is the natural endpoint of the afternoon's work — not a performance for its own sake.

**Reflection — 3 questions (spoken, age-8 wording):**
1. *When today did you forget about time because you were having so much fun?* (→ Natural Pull / flow)
2. *What did you do today that surprised you — something you didn't know you'd be good at?* (→ Discovery)
3. *Who in your family is happy because of something you made?* (→ Purpose)

Her answers are her own input to the updated Element Hypothesis. The Guide does not show her §3.

**Cue card — "What I'll tell Mom & Dad"** (3 sentence-starters to rehearse at 17:00):
1. "Today I was the **Director** of our Sparkle Squad, and my job was ______."
2. "My favorite thing I made was ______ **because** ______."
3. "The trickiest part was ______, and I **solved it by** ______."

## 12. Notes & Risks
*Design decisions for this one-day family run.*

- **One loop, not a shrunken week.** The afternoon runs the full loop once — commit, learn, build, test, improve, present, reflect — rather than sampling fragments.
- **Scope guardrails are load-bearing.** Three artifacts, kept small: one snack, one bracelet per person, one 3-zone course. Stated at results-setting and enforced before the showcase. A one-day project fails by scope, not concept.
- **Toddler integration is a feature, not a risk to manage away.** Giving the 3-year-old a real (sensory, safe) job is where Yesui's leadership and Purpose get exercised. No tiny beads; constant supervision near the kitchen.
- **Parents as fresh audience is structural.** It is what makes 5:30 a genuine reveal and protects the Discovery diagnostic. If a parent must hold the Guide role during the day, they step fully back into the audience for the showcase.
- **Element signals over output quality.** Success is not a perfect bracelet. Success is six hours of genuine work that tells us who Yesui is. The artifacts are the vehicle; the Element signals are the point.
